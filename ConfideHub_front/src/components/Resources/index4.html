Professional Support

Finding the Right Professional Help

While peer support and self-help tools are valuable, sometimes professional assistance is needed. Here are resources to help you find appropriate professional support.

Types of Mental Health Professionals

  • Psychiatrists (medical doctors who can prescribe medication)
  • Psychologists (provide therapy and psychological testing)
  • Licensed Counselors and Therapists
  • Social Workers
  • Psychiatric Nurses

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Persistent feelings of sadness or anxiety
  • Difficulty functioning in daily life
  • Thoughts of harming yourself or others
  • Substance use concerns
  • Major life transitions or trauma

Therapy FAQ

What should I expect in my first therapy session?

Your first session typically involves discussing your background, current concerns, and treatment goals. The therapist will explain their approach and answer your questions.

How do I know if therapy is working?

Progress in therapy often includes feeling better understood, gaining new insights, developing better coping skills, and seeing positive changes in your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes, with limited exceptions. Therapists maintain confidentiality except in cases where there is risk of harm to yourself or others, or when required by law (such as suspected abuse of a child or vulnerable adult).
