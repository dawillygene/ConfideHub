Emergency Support & Crisis Hotlines

If you or someone you know is experiencing a mental health crisis, these resources provide immediate support:

International Crisis Lines

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (US)

    1-800-273-8255 - 24/7 support

  • Crisis Text Line

    Text HOME to 741741 - 24/7 support

  • International Association for Suicide Prevention

    Visit findahelpline.com for local resources

Tanzania-Specific Resources

  • Tanzania Depression Foundation

    +255 716 666 516 - Support for depression

  • Mental Health Association of Tanzania

    +255 754 284 530 - General mental health support

  • Basic Needs Tanzania

    +255 222 780 541 - Mental health services

If you're in immediate danger, please call emergency services (911, 999, 112) for your country.

Professional Support Options

Finding The Right Help

Professional support can be a crucial part of mental health care. Here are resources to help you find qualified mental health professionals in your area.

Therapy Options

  • In-person therapy
  • Online counseling services
  • Group therapy sessions
  • Specialized treatment programs

Types of Mental Health Professionals

  • Psychiatrists (medical doctors who can prescribe medication)
  • Psychologists (specialized in psychological testing and therapy)
  • Licensed Counselors (trained in talk therapy approaches)
  • Social Workers (focus on connecting to community resources)

Low-Cost & Affordable Options

Mental health care should be accessible to everyone. Here are resources for affordable support options.

  • Community Health Centers

    Many offer sliding scale fees based on income.

  • University Training Clinics

    Supervised students provide low-cost services.

  • Online Therapy Platforms

    Many offer reduced rates compared to traditional therapy.

  • Non-profit Organizations

    Many provide free or low-cost mental health services.

Common Mental Health Conditions

Depression

Depression affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities. Symptoms can include persistent sadness, loss of interest, changes in appetite, and more.

Learn more about depression

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve excessive worry or fear that interferes with daily activities. Includes generalized anxiety, panic disorder, and specific phobias.

Learn more about anxiety

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. Symptoms include flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety.

Learn more about PTSD

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder causes unusual shifts in mood, energy, activity levels, and concentration. Features both manic and depressive episodes.

Learn more about bipolar disorder
